# Phase Evasion

*Phase Evasion* is a fast‑paced, reflex‑driven game built specifically for the *LumenLab*. It combines simple controls with escalating chaos, asking the player to survive as long as possible while navigating an ever‑changing field of hazards and opportunities.

At its core, Phase Evasion is about situational awareness, timing, and restraint. The rules are easy to understand, but surviving the game requires focus and adaptability as the difficulty ramps up.

---

## Summary

You control a color-shifting specter, free to move along the LED strip. Dangerous obstacles (called *flares*) appear, gliding toward you. If you collide with a flare, game over. In addition, bonus gem appear on random spots of the arena, which reward you double points upon capture, assuming you don't collide with any flares.

The longer you survive, the flares become faster and more dense, testing your hand-eye coordination.

#### Primary Objective

Survive for as long as possible earning the highest score you can from flare evasions and gem captures. There is no final level. Phase Evasion ends when you make a mistake. Keyword: *when*.

## How to Play

### Controls

When flares are approaching you:
* Press △ to phase shift to green.
* Press ○ to phase shift to red.
* Press ╳ to phase shift to blue
* Press ▢ to phase shift to yellow.

Gems will appear at random intervals. Capture them before they time out and move to another location
* Use the left analog Ⓛ joystick to move your specter across the LED strip.
* Capture as many gems as you safely can without colliding with any of the flare.
* All gems captured will reward you with 2x bonus points.

### Scoring

Your final score is calculated as the number of flares you evaded, plus double the number of gems captured.
> If you complete a game, finishing with:
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;10 flares evaded, and
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;3 gems captured.
>
> Then your score would be 16, calculated from 10 + (2 × 3).

## Design Philosophy

Phase Evasion is intentionally:

* Visually minimal. No distractions, just information
* Forces you to balance calculated risks vs rewards
* Hard to master. Panic and greed are your enemies

The LED format forces clarity. You always know *what* killed you and *why*.
